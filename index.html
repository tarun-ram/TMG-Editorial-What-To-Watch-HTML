What to Watch

 
 
 

Don’t panic, you haven’t accidentally slept for six months. It’s just discombobulating to see a bonus series of I’m a Celebrity, Get Me Out Of Here!, which normally runs every autumn, bestride the spring schedules. But before a certain other Coronation, who’s hoping to be anointed the first ever "I'm a Celebrity Legend"?

 
 

Picks of the week

By TV Critic
Michael Hogan

Which 'all star' contestant will get the crown? CREDIT: ITV

I'm a Celebrity... South Africa | ITV1, 9pm tonight
Marking the reality favourite’s 20th anniversary, this “all-stars” edition finds memorable former campmates returning to the jungle – this time in South Africa, rather than usual destination of Australia. Former cricketer Phil Tufnell is tipped to win, while the likes of Carol Vorderman, Paul Burrell, Janice Dickinson and Shaun Ryder promise plenty of talking points. It’s pre-recorded rather than live, which loses some of the unpredictability but means the episodes are more pacy; Bushtucker Trials are bigger and tougher. Hosts Ant and Dec are on hilarious form. Expect surprise arrivals and cruel plot twists.

 
 

The Mysterious Mr Lagerfeld | BBC Two, 9pm on Wednesday
This film peeps behind the sunglasses to tell the Chanel fashion designer’s story. Lagerfeld's cat Choupette proves a scene-stealer.

 

Dalgleish | Channel 5, 9pm on Thursday & Friday
I have fond memories of Roy Marsden as PD James’s cerebral detective but Bertie Carvel has taken up the mantle beautifully as the classy reboot returns.

Reviews

Succession | Sky Atlantic

After the last two turbulent episodes, there's a welcome return to awkward comedy – and a rare show of unity from the Roy siblings.

 

Malpractice | ITV1

Niamh Algar is excellent in this medical thriller as an A&E doctor who is put under investigation when a patient dies during a busy shift.

 

Little Richard: King and Queen of Rock ’n’ Roll | BBC

BBC Two's documentary persuasively argues that musical pioneer Little Richard was the rightful holder of the title normally given to Elvis.

 

The Diplomat | Netflix

Imagine a cross between Homeland and Emily in Paris and you'll land at this political thriller, which stars Keri Russell as a US diplomat sent to work in London.
